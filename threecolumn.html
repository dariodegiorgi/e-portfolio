About me

Dario De Giorgi

I chose this course because this field matches my ambitions with regard to my professional future. After working more than four years in the IT service of a private bank, I have realised that cyber security is probably one of the most essential needs in the financial industry. Many of the modules proposed in this course would be a major asset not only to myself, but also to the company that I currently work for. There is no doubt in my mind that a lot of the thematic proposed will also help me to bring to light certain security issues.

The reason I decided to do my master online, was mainly because I needed to have some flexibility with my actual job, since I am still an employee, I consider it to be more appropriate to keep my job, not only for obvious financial reasons, but also to further my career within my present employment. Studying after work and on weekends shouldn’t be an issue as I am single so don’t have any family ties or attachments for the time-being.

I am hoping that this degree will help me expand my knowledge and skill-set to further my path in the world of Information Technologies, and in this area in particular, which is, as far as I can see, one of the most important, and probably essential areas of IT. At the moment, we are under a lot of pressure to improve the security of different products within the bank, so it will be useful for me to apply what I learn and increase the robustness of our products. This degree will also help me improve my chances to apply for a job in the cyber security department of the bank.

Within my relatively short professional career, I have managed to gain an overview of the IT infrastructure and have already witnessed too many possible security breaches. I have had the chance to work in different areas, and travel to the other side of the world to coordinate several projects, installing servers in a data centre, and migrating traders’ telephone systems onto a new platform, giving support and assistance to the local IT teams in Hong Kong and Singapore. The different areas I worked in definitely gave me enough work experience to help me focus on which path I do or do not want to follow. It is now clear to me that a specialisation in cybersecurity would help me to move forward in my career.
